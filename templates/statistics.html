Anaphylaxis

Anaphylaxis: a serious allergic reaction that is rapid in onset and may cause death. It typically causes more than one of the following: an itchy rash, throat or tongue swelling, shortness of breath, vomiting, lightheadedness, and low blood pressure. These symptoms typically come on over minutes to hours.

Segment of young people with allergy that may lead to Anaphylaxis on joining primary school

The map shows rate of children whose parents report that their child has and allergy that may lead to anaphylaxis on entry to primary school.

As the map shows, children in urban and far west area had higher anaphylaxis rate.

Parents in urban and far west area of Victoria should pay more attention in their children’s food, living environment and medication to avoid the danger.

Gender differnce of Anaphylaxis

The following graph shows the anaphylaxis rate for boys and girls in Victoria. Although, the overal trend of Anaphylaxis rate is decrease in recent years, but boys always have higher anaphylaxis rate than girls. 

Therefore, boys’ parents should pay more attention on their kids allergy problem to avoid the risk.

Anaphylaxis rate for indigenous

Although, the overal trend for Victoria is decrease, the anaphylaxis rate of indigenous children is fluctuate increase in past few years.

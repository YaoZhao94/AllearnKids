Allergy

What is allergy?

Allergy is:

Allergy occurs when a person's reacts to substances in the environment that are harmless to most people. These substances are known as allergens and are found in dust mites, pets, pollen, insects, ticks, moulds, foods and some medications.

Food allergy

What is food allergy?

Food allergy is:

Food allergy is an immune system reaction that occurs soon after eating a certain food. Even a tiny amount of the allergy-causing food can trigger signs and symptoms such as digestive problems, hives or swollen airways.

EpiPen

What is an EpiPen?

EpiPen is:

EpiPen is the brand name of an auto-injectable device that delivers the drug epinephrine. It is a life-saving medication used when someone is experiencing a severe allergic reaction, known as anaphylaxis.

EpiPen

How to use an EpiPen?

Steps to use an EpiPen:

  1. Form fist around EpiPen and PULL OFF BLUE SAFETY RELEASE
  2. Hold leg still and PLACE ORANGE END against outer mid-thigh
  3. PUSH DOWN HARD untial a click is heard or felt and hold for 3 seconds

Anaphylaxis

What is Anaphylaxis?

Anaphylaxis is:

a potentially life threatening, severe allergic reaction and should always be treated as a medical emergency. Anaphylaxis occurs after exposure to an allergen (usually to foods, insects or medicines), to which a person is allergic.
